None of us like to see someone getting hurt. And noone wants to add to their pain. Unfortunately, when fights are uploaded and shared online, it makes things worse and fuels more violence. More people see what happened to them, including friends and family, which can be embarrassing.

It is a permanent reminder of what happened and they are linked to the fight forever. Having it shared can make it even harder to get over the incident.

Some people find it difficult to go to school or concentrate when they are at school. Some lose interest in socialising with their friends and having fun. Some are scared they will be hurt again.

Sharing or liking fight videos rewards those filming by giving them an audience and helping it grow and encourages those starting fights to do it again. If you see one, just scroll past it!

Five reasons to not share fights

  • It fuels more fights
  • It encourages the bullies
  • More kids get hurt
  • It's against the law
  • You want school to be safe

How should I deal with fight videos?

  • Scroll past them
  • Don't share them with friends
  • Report them as inappropriate
  • Tell friends sharing is illegal
  • Suggest other videos to share
